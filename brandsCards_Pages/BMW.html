What does word BMW mean?.

The full name of BMW is Bayerische Motoren Werke, which translates to Bavarian Motor Works in English. As the above would indicate, the name reflects the company's origin in the German state of Bavaria, and the initials of the German name happen to be the same in English. Today, the name BMW is widely recognized and celebrated around the world and has become synonymous with luxury, high-quality, high-performance vehicles..

What is the Meaning Behind the BMW Logo??

The BMW logo was first introduced in 1917, and it has undergone several changes over the years. However, the blue and white colors have remained a constant feature of the logo. For many years, there was a popular myth that the blue and white colors of the BMW logo were meant to represent a spinning propeller, in honor of the company's origins as an aircraft engine manufacturer. However, this is not entirely accurate. For BMW, the blue and white colors of the BMW logo symbolized the Bavarian flag colors and represented the company’s origin in Bavaria, Germany.

It's worth noting that the quarters of the inner circle on the BMW logo display the state colors of the State of Bavaria – white and blue. But they are in the inverse order (at least as far as heraldic rules are concerned, where you read clockwise from the top left). The reason for this inverse order of blue and white in the BMW logo was the local trademark law at the time, which forbade the use of state coats of arms or other symbols of sovereignty on commercial logos.
