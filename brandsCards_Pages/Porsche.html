PORSCHE

The Porsche brand is named after its founder, Ferdinand Porsche.


The Porsche brand was founded in 1931 in Stuttgart, Germany, by Ferdinand Porsche and has since become one of the most iconic and recognizable luxury car brands in the world.
What is the Meaning of the Word Porsche?
The word "Porsche" is a surname of Austrian-German origin, and it does not have a specific meaning in German or any other language. The founder of the Porsche car company, Ferdinand Porsche, had the surname "Porsche." The name "Porsche" is simply a family name that has become associated with the iconic luxury car brand that Ferdinand Porsche founded in 1931 in Stuttgart, Germany.

Design

Why does the Porsche logo have a Horse?

The Porsche logo is inspired by Stuttgart's city seal, showcasing a rearing horse within a shield shape. Reflecting the city's equestrian heritage, the logo symbolizes the power, agility, and elegance of Porsche cars. The rearing horse signifies speed and energy, aligning with the company's commitment to high-performance sports cars. The logo also features antlers and red-black stripes, influenced by the coat of arms of Baden-Württemberg, honoring the region where Porsche's headquarters are situated and its cultural heritage.

What is the Meaning of the Porsche logo?

The Porsche logo was designed by Franz Xaver Reimspiess, a senior designer at Porsche, drawing inspiration from the Coats of Arms of Stuttgart and Baden-Württemberg. The shield-shaped emblem, first registered in 1952, symbolizes the company's roots and commitment to quality and dynamism. The design incorporates a rearing horse from Stuttgart's city seal, reflecting the city's history in horse-breeding since its founding in 950AD. Though Porsche's founder Ferdinand Porsche didn't witness its widespread use, the logo debuted on the Porsche 356's steering wheel horn in 1952.
